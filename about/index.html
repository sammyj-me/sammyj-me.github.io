A few goals for my 20s

Become better at putting myself out there. I have always struggled with talking about myself and my interests with other people, and this has led to a few less-than fulfilling friendships.

This website is a big step for me in taking action to muddle through to my goal of "putting myself out there" with the idea that the energy I put out to the world is the energy that I will receive back.

A little experiment on the ol' platitude if you would.

It's almost counter culture within my social group in this day and age (as of 6/30/2021) to post more on the internet. What I fear is coming across as self-centered, but I think with the right intent and content (making sure there isn't a disconnect between good intentions and "bad" actions), one can project an "authentic self" that others can connect with on social media. Whatever "authentic" means anyway. Posting more on social media isn't the end-all-be-all for this goal, but it is a step forward in getting me used to talking about myself in a "public setting."

Travel More. I spend a lot of time on the internet taking peeks into other peoples' lives, and with true independence right around the corner, I will now have the opportunity to bridge the gap between imaginative lands, tastes, and human perspectives into visceral ones. One thought I always go back to is that there's always something to say about experiencing things in person vs. from experiencing it second hand. The contextual backdrop of getting in your car, boarding a plane, and having your senses stimulated by the new sights and sounds arguably provides a more "real" experience than just reading about it. From travelling in my 20s, I hope to learn to internalize the beauty of the present moment, with complete awareness of my senses, just a little bit more.
